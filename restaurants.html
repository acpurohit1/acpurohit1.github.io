restaurants



top picks

Here are my picks for the best of the best (Crowdsourced section coming soon since I'm missing seafood!)

Traveller's Thali House (Beacon Hill)
Authentic Indian food served Thali style by the hippie owner who lived there for 20 years! Must try the pani puri.

Pie Bar (Capitol Hill)
I don't even like pie but I can't get enough. Sweet and savory pie by the slice (Deserted Island is a must.)

Pink Door (Downtown)
Cozy, beautiful, and during Christmastime, magical. Go when the live jazz band is playing if you can

Petra (Belltown)
Get the Taste of Africa, a garlicky Jordanian goodness made with imported spices

Kylie's Pizza (Fremont)
Deep dish deliciousness with a sweet, chunky tomato sauce

Din Tai Fung (Bellevue)
Watch the cooks make dumplings from scratch through the window while you wait (go early!)

8oz Burger Bar (Capitol Hill)
Super hipster, super delicious. I love the Portland ketchup

quick meals

Homegrown (Capitol Hill)
Warm baked bread, seasonal creative sandwiches like pumpkin or cranberry turkey

Kadai Makan (Capitol Hill)
Malaysian street food

Old School Frozen Custard (Capitol Hill)
In between frozen yogurt and ice cream, but better

Paseo's (Fremont)
Super famous. Go early or else bread runs out, or call ahead to pick up your order in person, no wait (!)

Aladdin's (University District)
Best gyro in Seattle

Cedar's (University District)
Authentic old Greek restaurant, owned by an old Greek woman

Rocco's (Belltown)
Pizza by the slice, perfectly crispy

brunch


Essential Baking Co. (Wallingford)
Fresh, healthy, delicious salads, sandwiches, and baked goods

Macrina Bakery (Belltown)
Named as one of top 10 bread bakeries by Bon Appetit

Hi Spot Cafe (Madrona)
A house-turned-restaurant. Long wait for a reason

Portage Bay Cafe (SLU, UDistrict)
Go in summer for the unlimited fresh berry bar

Coastal Kitchen (Capitol Hill)
Excellent banana pancakes and rotating specials (last one: lamb, sweet potatoes, onions, and pita - delicious)

Cafe Petirosso (Capitol Hill)
Quaint, brunchy cafe. Excellent chicken sandwich and brioche French toast

Bakery Nouveau (Capitol Hill)
Renowned twice-baked chocolate and almond croissants

Skillet Diner (Capitol Hill)
Started out as popular food truck. Go in a small group

Saley's Crepes (Downtown)
Looks sketchy but Nutella banana crepe is phenomenal

Boat Street Cafe (Interbay)
Chic but cozy, modern yet casual

La Reve (Queen Anne)
Renowned for their kouign amann, a sweet/buttery muffin-meets-croissant

Cafe Flora (Madison Valley)
Vegan/vegetarian restaurant that even carnivores will love. Indoor garden area

cafes


Bauhaus (Capitol Hill)
Books and hipsters. A Cap Hill classic

Espresso Vivace (Capitol Hill)
Has a "quiet room" in the back for complete silence

Volunteer Park Cafe (Capitol Hill)
Cozy communal feel. Get the pear cardamom muffin!

Starbucks on Olive (Capitol Hill)
Feels like a cozy lodge with a fireplace. Not your typical Starbucks

Ada's (Capitol Hill)
Cafe adjoined to a science and technical bookstore - my favorite

Fremont Coffee (Fremont)
An old house, each room with its own personality

sit-down


Mamnoon (Cap Hill)
Modern Middle Eastern cuisine, fresh unlimited warm pita bread. Try the Muhammara (pomegranate/walnut/cumin)

Machiavelli's (Capitol Hill)
Intimate setting, excellent Italian

Vios Cafe (Capitol Hill)
Authentic family-owned Greek food

Nue (Capitol Hill)
Street food from around the world. The Caribbean-spiced goat curry or Indian pakoras is a must

Barrio (Capitol Hill)
An entire wall of 100+ candles that takes 1 hour to light each night - gorgeous

Fogon (Capitol Hill)
Go-to place for margaritas and a good serving of guac

Terra Plata (Capitol Hill)
Check out Paella Mondays, 3 varieties, each $30

Via Tribunali (Capitol Hill)
Thin crust pizza you get to cut with pizza scissors!

Jamjuree (Capitol Hill)
Favorite Thai restaurant in Seattle

Ayutthaya (Capitol Hill)
Also great

How to Cook a Wolf (Queen Anne)
Rustic, dark, candlelit. Excellent fresh pasta

Cafe Turko (Fremont)
Try the hummus sampler (beet, spinach, yam, normal)

Kwanjai Thai (Fremont)
Thai in a house converted to a restaurant

Harbor City (International District)
The better of the dim sum places

Local 360 (Belltown)
Rustic decor, locally sourced ingredients

5 Point Cafe (Belltown)

Zeek's Pizza (multiple locations)
Doughy, thick crust and saucy pizza
