outdoors




Waterfront trail
Lots of walkers and bikers, gorgeous waterfront views. Look out for the Rose Garden!

Japanese Garden
Beautiful, peaceful, and expansive. Check out the turtles and colorful koi fish in the pond

Hikes
Here are some of my favorites:

  • Mailbox - Said to be one of the hardest hikes in the PNW. But there's a mailbox at the top!

  • Mt. Si - Moderate difficulty, rock scramble at the end for some beautiful views

  • Snow Lake - Relatively easy, emerges out to beautiful lake surrounded by mountains

  • Bandera Mountain - Challenging, very uphill for most of the way. Rock scramble

  • Wallace Falls - Waterfall hike! Popular hike but not overcrowded, or very strenuous

  • Lake Serene - Another gorgeous lake hike
Rainier/Paradise
Gorgeous wildflowers in late summer, or Reflection Lake trail (so beautiful) any time of year

Ohanapecosh
Near Rainier, good for groups and low-key car camping

Lake Union
Great for kayaking; you can even kayak to Gasworks!

Alki Beach
The most beautiful view of Seattle. Go at night to see the Seattle skyline reflecting in the water

Golden Gardens
Bonfires and beach fun. Go early to get a pit or make friends and share with others. Gorgeous sunsets

Ski resorts

  • Steven's Pass - Good variety of runs but can get super crowded

  • Mt. Baker - My favorite. Shorter lines, awesome runs. Great for a weekend trip

  • Crystal - Harder runs than Stevens, gondola to get to backside

  • Snoqualmie - Easier runs, more family-oriented.

Green Lake
Great lake for picnics

UW Cherry Blossoms
Dozens of cherry blossom trees in full bloom around June. Go early in the day to avoid crowds
